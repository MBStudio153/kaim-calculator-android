The Knowledge
Capital Ledger

KAIM stands for Knowledge Asset Impairment Model. It finds the R&D value hidden off the balance sheet by treating the R&D department as the factory floor — its output measured in the cash returns it produces, not in the year it was expensed. The results of that re-accounting are reflected in the model's KAIM Book Value below. The model can be applied to any publicly traded company.

Enter your own company's metrics to see how its balance sheet reads once the knowledge factory is put back on the books. This app runs entirely in your browser — no data is sent anywhere, and it works fully offline once loaded.

Impairment Trigger Tests

Trigger 1 — Innovation Stagnation

R&D-to-revenue ratio has declined materially for 3+ consecutive years with no major product cycle.

Trigger 2 — Platform Abandonment

A core platform or product line the KCS was built on has been discontinued, sunset, or replaced by a competitor's standard.

Trigger 3 — Competitive Displacement

Sustained revenue or unit decline (e.g. YoY contraction) signaling the knowledge base has lost market relevance.

KAIM — Knowledge Asset Impairment Model. This calculator is an independent, unofficial implementation of the KAIM methodology described in "Evaluating Tech Stocks Using the Knowledge Asset Impairment Model" by Matt Bernau. It is provided for educational and illustrative purposes only and does not constitute financial, investment, legal, or accounting advice. KAIM is a research lens, not an audited accounting standard or a validated forecasting tool. Consult a licensed financial advisor and audited financial statements before making any investment decision.

Note on CPI adjustment: the source e-book applies a CPI-U inflation adjustment to restate each year's R&D spend into constant dollars before summing the Knowledge Capital Stock. That step has been omitted from this app for simplicity; the Knowledge Capital Stock here reflects nominal (as-reported) R&D spend only. The full CPI-adjusted methodology remains documented in the e-book.
