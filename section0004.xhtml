Appendix 1: Magnificent 7 KAIM Analysis — FY2025

(NVIDIA covered in main publication. Six remaining companies analyzed below.)

 

Forward Asset Methodology Note: Unlike NVIDIA, most Mag 7 companies do not disclose a single, clean, consolidated forward purchase commitment figure comparable to NVIDIA's chip backlog and cloud agreement disclosures. Forward assets are added to KAIM Book Value only where a company's FY2025 10-K disclosed a clear, auditable total: Apple ($71.0B in manufacturing and other purchase obligations) and Alphabet ($149.1B in total purchase commitments). Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, and Tesla either do not disclose a single comparable total or report fragmented figures across quarters that cannot be reliably reconstructed for FY2025 year-end without making material assumptions. For these four companies, KAIM Book Value reflects GAAP equity plus KCS only — consistent with the conservative principle that KAIM should not estimate what a company has not clearly disclosed.

 

 

Microsoft (MSFT) | Technology | FY End: June 30, 2025

What Was Analyzed

Microsoft was analyzed as the most diversified knowledge factory in the Mag 7. The KAIM model capitalizes 20 years of R&D spanning the transition from Windows-era software to Azure cloud infrastructure, Microsoft 365 Copilot, Xbox/Activision gaming, and enterprise AI. With a KCS of ~$338B nominal, Microsoft's GAAP P/B of ~9x collapses dramatically under KAIM — revealing one of the most asset-rich companies in the world by honest accounting.

Data note: FY ends June 30. KCS uses FY2006–FY2025 window.

Apple (AAPL) | Technology | FY End: Sept 27, 2025

What Was Analyzed

Apple was analyzed as a paradox: the world's most valuable company by market cap yet one of the lowest GAAP book values among the Mag 7, primarily because decades of buybacks have driven retained earnings negative. KAIM corrects both distortions simultaneously — adding $245B in nominal KCS from 20 years of R&D across iPhone, Mac Silicon, AirPods, Apple Intelligence, and the M-series chip family, plus $26.0B in forward purchase obligations disclosed in Apple's FY2025 10-K.

Data note: FY ends late September. Negative retained earnings from buybacks reduce GAAP book dramatically. Forward assets sourced from Apple's FY2025 10-K (filed Oct 2025): manufacturing purchase obligations $56.2B, other purchase obligations $14.8B — the only Mag 7 company analyzed here with a clean, fully disclosed total.

Alphabet (GOOGL) | Technology | FY End: Dec 31, 2025

What Was Analyzed

Alphabet was analyzed as the company with arguably the deepest knowledge factory in the world — Search, Maps, YouTube, Android, DeepMind, Waymo, and Google Cloud spanning two decades of compounding R&D. At $61B in FY2025 R&D spend, Alphabet is the second-largest R&D spender globally. KAIM also adds $29.8B in forward infrastructure commitments disclosed in Alphabet's FY2025 10-K, the largest forward asset addition outside NVIDIA. The model reveals a KAIM P/B of ~2.4x, suggesting Alphabet may be the most conservatively priced knowledge asset in the Mag 7.

Data note: Google IPO 2004; using FY2006+ for 20yr window. Forward assets sourced from Alphabet's FY2025 10-K: total purchase commitments of $149.1B (technical infrastructure, inventory orders, energy contracts, content licenses), of which $113.0B is short-term. No separate cloud/hardware split is disclosed, so the full figure is treated as infrastructure (80% recognition, 25% cost basis) consistent with NVIDIA's chip backlog treatment.

Tesla (TSLA) | Automotive/Energy | FY End: Dec 31, 2025

What Was Analyzed

Tesla receives the smallest KAIM correction of any Mag 7 company — its KCS window is only 14 years (FY2012–FY2025) and annual R&D spend remained under $7B through FY2025. The analysis focuses heavily on the Trigger 3 CAUTION flag: FY2025 revenue declined 3% YoY and vehicle deliveries fell 9% — the first sustained reversal in Tesla's growth history. Tesla's KAIM P/B of ~14x remains the highest among all six, reflecting genuine optionality for FSD, Optimus robotics, and energy storage.

Data note: Tesla founded 2003; KCS uses FY2012–2025 (14yr window, first full 10-K available).
