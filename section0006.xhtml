All R&D expense figures for NVIDIA, Microsoft, Apple, Alphabet, Meta, Amazon, Tesla, Johnson & Johnson, JPMorgan Chase, Walmart, ExxonMobil, UnitedHealth Group, Procter & Gamble, and Berkshire Hathaway for fiscal years FY2006–FY2025. Retrieved from sec.gov/edgar. Specific filings: Income Statement line 'Research and Development Expense' (or equivalent classification).

MacroTrends — Historical Financials

Cross-reference source for R&D expense history and shareholders' equity figures. Used to verify SEC EDGAR data across all 14 companies. Retrieved from macrotrends.net.

Companies Market Cap

Market capitalization figures at fiscal year-end dates for all 14 companies. Retrieved from companiesmarketcap.com. Figures used: NVIDIA Jan 2025, Microsoft Jun 2025, Apple Sep 2025, Alphabet/Meta/Amazon/Tesla Dec 2025, and all Appendix 2 companies at their respective FY-end dates.

Company Annual Reports

Supplementary data for forward contract assets (hardware purchase commitments, cloud service agreements), segment revenue figures, and product roadmap information. Sources: NVIDIA FY2025 10-K (Form 10-K filed with SEC, fiscal year ending January 26, 2025), and equivalent filings for all other companies.

 

Inflation Adjustment

 

U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — CPI-U

Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U), All Items, Not Seasonally Adjusted. Series ID: CUUR0000SA0. Retrieved from bls.gov/cpi. January index values used for each fiscal year deflator calculation.

Example: Jan 2025 index: 317.67. Applied to all 14 companies across 20-year R&D windows.

Deflator Methodology

Each year's nominal R&D multiplied by (CPI_Jan2025 / CPI_Jan[year]). Years prior to FY2006 use the earliest available index value. Real KCS = sum of all CPI-adjusted annual R&D figures over the 20-year window.

Example: FY2006 deflator (Jan 2006 CPI: 198.3): 317.67 / 198.3 = 1.602x

Conceptual Foundations & Related Literature

 

Lev, B. & Sougiannis, T. (1996)

"The Capitalization, Amortization, and Value-Relevance of R&D." Journal of Accounting and Economics, 21(1), 107–138.

Foundational empirical study demonstrating that R&D expenditures have value-relevant information content and that capitalized R&D improves earnings predictability.

Lev, B. & Zarowin, P. (1999)

"The Boundaries of Financial Reporting and How to Extend Them." Journal of Accounting Research, 37(2), 353–385.

Documents the declining relevance of earnings and book value under GAAP for firms with high intangible intensity.

Corrado, C., Hulten, C. & Sichel, D. (2009)

"Intangible Capital and U.S. Economic Growth." Review of Income and Wealth, 55(3), 661–685.

Broad macroeconomic treatment of intangible capital — including R&D, software, and organizational capital — as productive investment omitted from national accounts.

Peters, R. & Taylor, L. (2017)

"Intangible Capital and the Investment-q Relation." Journal of Financial Economics, 123(2), 251–272.

Proposes a method for capitalizing R&D and SG&A to construct firm-level intangible capital stock. Direct methodological antecedent to the KCS construction in KAIM.

Lev, B. (2018)

"The Deteriorating Usefulness of Financial Report Information and How to Reverse It." Accounting and Business Research, 48(5), 465–493.

Reviews the growing failure of GAAP to capture value for knowledge-intensive companies and proposes accounting reforms.

FASB ASC 730

Financial Accounting Standards Board — Accounting Standards Codification Topic 730: Research and Development. Governs the immediate expensing of R&D under U.S. GAAP.

The specific rule KAIM restates. Under ASC 730, all R&D costs must be charged to expense when incurred, with limited exceptions for acquired in-process R&D in business combinations.

IAS 38 (IFRS)

International Accounting Standard 38: Intangible Assets. Under IFRS, development costs meeting specific criteria may be capitalized; research costs must be expensed.

Contrast to U.S. GAAP: IFRS permits limited capitalization of development-phase spending, providing a partial international precedent for KAIM's approach.

 

Disclaimers & Limitations

 

1.  All KAIM figures are analytical reconstructions for illustrative and educational purposes only. They do not represent audited financial statements and have not been reviewed or endorsed by any of the companies analyzed.

2.  R&D expense data for JPMorgan Chase, Walmart, UnitedHealth Group, and Berkshire Hathaway is estimated from disclosed technology investment figures, as these companies do not report a formal R&D line item. These proxies introduce additional uncertainty relative to companies with explicit R&D disclosure.

3.  Market capitalization figures reflect fiscal year-end dates and may differ from figures at other measurement dates. NVIDIA's market capitalization is referenced as of January 2025 (fiscal year end), which preceded significant subsequent price movements.

4.  The 20-year productive life assumption is a modelling choice, not an audited useful life determination. Actual economic useful lives of specific R&D investments may be shorter or longer. The impairment trigger framework is designed to surface cases where this assumption is materially incorrect.

5.  This publication does not constitute investment advice. Nothing herein should be construed as a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any security. Past performance of any company's R&D investment or stock price is not indicative of future results.

6.  CPI-U deflators from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics are used as a proxy for R&D input cost inflation. Actual input cost inflation for engineering labor and compute resources may diverge from general CPI, particularly in periods of rapid AI-related demand for specialized talent and GPU compute.
